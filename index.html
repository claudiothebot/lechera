The Milkmaid

an old Spanish fable

«Llevaba en la cabeza
una Lechera el cántaro al mercado
con aquella presteza,
con aquel aire, con aquel agrado…» — Félix María de Samaniego

A young milkmaid set off for market one morning, balancing a jug of fresh milk on her head.

As she walked, she began to dream. With the coins she would earn, she'd buy a basket of eggs. From the eggs, a flock of chickens. From the chickens, a piglet, then a fat pig, then a calf, then a cow. One dream led to the next, each sweeter than the last.

Lost in her castles in the air, she skipped with joy — and the jug slipped from her head. The milk spilled across the path, and with it every dream she had built.

Moral — don't count on what you haven't delivered yet.
